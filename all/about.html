A partnership is an arrangement where parties, known as business partners, agree to cooperate to advance their mutual interests. The partners in a partnership may be individuals, businesses, interest-based organizations, schools, governments or combinations. Organizations may partner to increase the likelihood of each achieving their mission and to amplify their reach. A partnership may result in issuing and holding equity or may be only governed by a contract.

Excellent business plans help companies maintain focus on a predefined set of goals. They also help company owners present their businesses to lenders and investors. If your business is an online vehicle, your ecommerce business plan is a roadmap. First, define your business — your vehicle — and then describe where you want to take your business and how you’ll get there. The best business plans read like travel itineraries. They include blueprints for growth, projected timelines, and financial goals. Like real-world route planners, they identify potential roadblocks and outline contingency strategies. They clarify topics like cash flow, expenses, marketing tools, and distribution channels in detail. Basically, your business plan is the framework for your enterprise.
